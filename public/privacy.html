Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 13, 2026

1. Overview

This Privacy Policy explains how Musical Sight (https://piano.akash.fi/) collects and uses data.

The Service is operated by an individual developer. No personal accounts or direct personal data are collected.

2. Data Collected

The Service does not intentionally collect personally identifiable information (PII).

However, limited technical data is automatically collected for analytics and monitoring purposes, including:

  • IP address (processed temporarily)
  • Browser and device information
  • Pages visited and interactions
  • Performance metrics (e.g. load times)
  • Error and crash reports
  • Anonymous session identifiers

This data may constitute personal data under GDPR (e.g. IP address).

3. Purpose of Processing

Data is processed strictly to:

  • Ensure the Service functions correctly
  • Monitor performance and reliability
  • Detect and fix errors
  • Improve user experience
  • Maintain security

No profiling or user identification is performed.

4. Analytics Provider

The Service uses Grafana Faro via Grafana Cloud (Grafana Labs) for frontend observability.

This means analytics data is processed by Grafana Labs on their infrastructure.

More information:

  • https://grafana.com/oss/faro/
  • https://grafana.com/docs/grafana-cloud/monitor-applications/frontend-observability/settings-and-policies/data-privacy/

5. Legal Basis (GDPR)

Processing is based on:

  • Legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR) — to operate, secure, and improve the Service

Where required, analytics will only be enabled with user consent.

6. Data Retention

Analytics data is retained for up to 90 days, after which it is deleted or automatically removed.

7. Data Sharing

Data is not sold or used for advertising.

Data is shared only with:

  • Grafana Labs (analytics provider)
  • Necessary infrastructure providers

8. International Transfers

Data may be processed outside the EEA. Appropriate safeguards (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) are used where required.

9. Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access, correct, or delete your data
  • Object to or restrict processing
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

Due to the anonymous nature of the data, identifying specific data may not be possible.
